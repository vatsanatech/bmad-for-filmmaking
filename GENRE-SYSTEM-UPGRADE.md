## The 8 Genre-Specific Story Agents

### 1. **Suspense Architect** (Thriller/Suspense)
**Trained On**: Sujoy Ghosh, Sriram Raghavan, Anurag Kashyap

**Specializes In**:
- Mystery structure with multiple twists
- Atmospheric tension building
- Moral ambiguity and grey characters
- Neo-noir psychological thrillers
- "Journey over climax" philosophy

**Visual Language**: Close-ups, tight framing, darkness, shadows, suspense-building shots

**Example Films**: Kahaani, Andhadhun, Drishyam, Badlapur

---

### 2. **Romance Architect** (Romance/Romantic Drama)
**Trained On**: Yash Chopra, Sanjay Leela Bhansali, Imtiaz Ali

**Specializes In**:
- Romance as visual poetry
- Love against odds (family, society)
- Grand opulent aesthetics vs contemporary authenticity
- Emotional journey through music and visuals
- Tragic romance vs feel-good endings

**Visual Language**: Soft lighting, wide landscapes, flowing fabrics, intimate close-ups, golden hour

**Example Films**: Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge, Devdas, Bajirao Mastani, Jab We Met, Tamasha

---

### 3. **Action Architect** (Action/Action Thriller)
**Trained On**: Rohit Shetty, Siddharth Anand, Ali Abbas Zafar

**Specializes In**:
- Physics-defying spectacle (Shetty style)
- Sleek international action (Anand style)
- Grounded muscular heroism (Zafar style)
- Hero vs system narratives
- Action serving character and emotion

**Visual Language**: Wide shots for geography, movement choreography, explosion walks, slow-motion hero moments

**Example Films**: Singham, War, Pathaan, Sultan, Tiger franchise

---

### 4. **Comedy Architect** (Comedy/Comedy-Drama)
**Trained On**: Hrishikesh Mukherjee, Rajkumar Hirani, Priyadarshan

**Specializes In**:
- Gentle observational humor (Mukherjee)
- Social satire with purpose (Hirani)
- Escalating situational chaos (Priyadarshan)
- Comedy with heart and warmth
- Laugh-cry balance

**Visual Language**: Two-shots for reactions, wider for physical comedy, bright lighting, expressive faces

**Example Films**: Gol Maal, Chupke Chupke, 3 Idiots, Munna Bhai MBBS, Hera Pheri, Hungama

---

### 5. **Drama Architect** (Drama/Family Drama/Social Drama)
**Trained On**: Bimal Roy, Gulzar, Ashutosh Gowariker

**Specializes In**:
- Dignity of common people (Roy)
- Emotional complexity and poetry (Gulzar)
- Personal stories in epic scale (Gowariker)
- Restraint over melodrama
- Human transformation and catharsis

**Visual Language**: Natural light, intimate close-ups, observational camera, restraint, silence speaks

**Example Films**: Do Bigha Zamin, Mother India, Ijaazat, Piku, Lagaan, Swades

---

### 6. **Horror Architect** (Horror/Supernatural Thriller)
**Trained On**: Ram Gopal Varma, Vikram Bhatt, Vishal Bhardwaj

**Specializes In**:
- Atmospheric dread over jump-scares (RGV)
- Indian supernatural mythology (Bhatt)
- Human monsters and gothic darkness (Bhardwaj)
- Psychological vs supernatural horror
- Sound design as weapon

**Visual Language**: Darkness, shadows, long static shots, handheld POV, minimalist lighting, ambiguity

**Example Films**: Kaun?, Bhoot, Raaz, 1920, Tumbbad, Maqbool (dark)

---

### 7. **Musical Architect** (Musical/Dance Drama/Family Saga)
**Trained On**: Farah Khan, Karan Johar, Aditya Chopra

**Specializes In**:
- Dance as storytelling (Farah Khan)
- Family sagas with emotional maximalism (Karan Johar)
- Musical romance with NRI sensibility (Aditya Chopra)
- Spectacle with purpose
- Songs advancing narrative

**Visual Language**: Wide for choreography, grand production design, aspirational aesthetics, slow-motion emotion

**Example Films**: Om Shanti Om, Main Hoon Na, Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham, Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge, Kal Ho Naa Ho

---

### 8. **Social Cinema Architect** (Social/Parallel Cinema/Political Drama)
**Trained On**: Shyam Benegal, Govind Nihalani, Anurag Basu

**Specializes In**:
- Parallel cinema realism (Benegal)
- Institutional corruption critique (Nihalani)
- Contemporary social issues with beauty (Basu)
- Systemic examination, not individual blame
- Authenticity and dignity of subjects

**Visual Language**: Natural locations, handheld documentary style, minimal music, long takes, observational

**Example Films**: Ankur, Manthan, Nishant, Ardh Satya, Aakrosh, Life in a... Metro, Barfi!

## How the System Works

### 1. User Provides Concept
- Story idea (even vague)
- Target format (30 sec, 5 min, 15 min, feature)
- Genre/tone preference (if known)

### 2. Master Story Director Analyzes
Uses decision tree to identify:
- **Primary Genre** (60%+): Dominant storytelling mode
- **Secondary Elements** (20-40%): Flavors that enhance primary
- **Tonal Analysis**: Dark vs light, realistic vs stylized
- **Cultural Context**: Urban/rural, class, regional, period

### 3. Genre Agent Selected
Master Story Director selects appropriate specialist and briefs them with:
- User's original concept
- Genre classification and rationale
- Target format/length
- Specific elements requested
- Tonal requirements

### 4. Genre-Specific Story Development
Selected agent applies their Bollywood masters' techniques:
- Genre-specific structure
- Character types appropriate to genre
- Visual language conventions
- Dialogue style (Hindi-dominant when appropriate)
- Climax/resolution fitting genre

### 5. Quality Assurance
Master Story Director verifies:
- Genre conventions honored while surprising
- Cinema-grade quality (not generic)
- Bollywood masters' principles visible
- Language appropriate
- Ready for full development

## Shot-by-Shot Breakdown System

### NEW: Complete Production & AI Generation Ready

Every shot now includes:

#### Camera Specifications
- Lens, angle, height, movement
- Focus, aspect ratio, frame rate
- Framing and composition details

#### Lighting
- Type, time of day, direction, quality
- Color temperature, mood
- Key/fill/back light positions

#### Action & Blocking
- Character positions and movements
- Performance notes
- Continuity requirements

#### Sound
- Dialogue, effects, ambient, music
- Delivery style

#### Emotional & Storytelling
- Emotional objective
- Story purpose
- Visual subtext

#### AI Video Generation Prompt
Complete prompt ready for:
- Runway Gen-3
- Pika
- Sora
- Midjourney + Runway pipeline
- Any AI video tool

**Example AI Prompt Format**:
```
Shot Type: Wide establishing shot
Duration: 4 seconds
Aspect Ratio: 16:9

Visual Description: [Detailed setting, characters, objects, action]
Camera: [Angle, movement, framing, lens feel]
Lighting: [Time, quality, direction, mood, color]
Performance: [Character emotion, expression, body language, energy]
Style & Mood: [Genre, cinematography style, mood, color palette, references]
Technical: [Quality, realism, detail]

Negative Prompt: [Things to avoid]
```
