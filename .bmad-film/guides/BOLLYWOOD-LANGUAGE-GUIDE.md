# Bollywood Language Standards Guide
## For All BMAD-FILM Story Agents

**Version**: 1.0.0
**Purpose**: Define Bollywood-grade Hindi and regional dialect standards for all story agents

---

## Core Principle

> **DEFAULT LANGUAGE**: Hindi (simple, accessible, mass-market)
>
> Bollywood Hindi is NOT literary/formal Hindi. It's the Hindi people actually speak - simple vocabulary, natural rhythm, emotional, with English words woven in where natural.

---

## Bollywood Hindi Standards

### What is "Bollywood Hindi"?

**Bollywood Hindi** = Simple Hindi + Natural English mixing + Emotional expression + Mass accessibility

**NOT**:
- ❌ Literary/Shudh Hindi (too formal)
- ❌ Sanskrit-heavy vocabulary (inaccessible)
- ❌ Complex sentence structures
- ❌ Bookish dialogue no one actually speaks

**YES**:
- ✅ Simple everyday Hindi
- ✅ Emotional, dramatic expression
- ✅ Natural English words where Indians use them
- ✅ Regional flavor when appropriate
- ✅ Dialogue people actually say

## Hindi Vocabulary Levels

### LEVEL 1: Simple Bollywood Hindi (DEFAULT - Use This)

**Vocabulary**: Common words everyone knows
**English mixing**: Natural (tech terms, modern concepts, urban slang)
**Accessibility**: 100% - Every Hindi speaker understands

**Examples**:
- ✅ "मैं तुम्हें बहुत miss करता हूँ" / "Main tumhe bahut miss karta hoon"
- ✅ "Phone क्यों नहीं उठाया?" / "Phone kyun nahi uthaya?"
- ✅ "यह decision मुझे लेना है" / "Yeh decision mujhe lena hai"
- ✅ "College में क्या हुआ?" / "College mein kya hua?"
- ✅ "Sorry बोलो, बस" / "Sorry bolo, bas"

**This is Bollywood**: Watch any mainstream Hindi film - this is how people talk.

### LEVEL 2: Pure Hindi (Use Only When Character Demands)

**Vocabulary**: Hindi words for concepts
**English mixing**: Minimal
**Accessibility**: 80% - Some words less common

**Examples**:
- "मैं तुम्हारी याद में खोया रहता हूँ" (missing the English "miss")
- "दूरभाष क्यों नहीं उठाया?" (formal "telephone")
- Only use for: Period films, literary characters, formal situations

### LEVEL 3: Shudh/Sanskrit Hindi (Almost Never Use)

**Vocabulary**: Sanskrit-origin, formal, literary
**English mixing**: None
**Accessibility**: 40% - Many won't understand

**Examples**:
- "मैं तुम्हारे वियोग में व्याकुल हूँ"
- TOO FORMAL - Don't use unless specific character (pandit, scholar)

### LEVEL 4: Hinglish (Urban/Youth contexts)

**Vocabulary**: Heavy English mixing
**English mixing**: 50%+
**Accessibility**: Urban youth, educated class

**Examples**:
- "Yaar, I'm so confused यार"
- "This is too much, seriously"
- Use for: Urban characters, younger generation, comedy

## Code-Switching Rules (Hindi + English)

### When to Use English Words (Natural Bollywood Style)

**ALWAYS mix English for**:
1. **Modern technology**: Phone, laptop, computer, internet, SMS, email, WiFi, app, WhatsApp
2. **Professional terms**: Office, meeting, boss, client, project, deadline, presentation
3. **Urban infrastructure**: Police station, hospital, airport, mall, metro, cafe, hotel
4. **Education**: School, college, university, exam, degree, professor, class
5. **Contemporary concepts**: Boyfriend, girlfriend, date, party, selfie, status, post
6. **Casual expressions**: Sorry, thank you, please, OK, bye, hi, hello, cool, nice

**Examples of Natural Mixing**:
```
✅ "Police station में Inspector से baat karo"
✅ "Office का kaam bahut hai"
✅ "WhatsApp पे message भेज दो"
✅ "College के baad café चलें?"
✅ "Sorry यार, galti ho gayi"
```

**This is authentic**: Real Indians speak like this. This is Bollywood Hindi.

### When to Keep Pure Hindi

**Use Hindi for**:
1. **Emotions**: Love, anger, pain, joy - Hindi is more emotional
2. **Family relations**: Maa, papa, bhai, behen, dadi, nani
3. **Cultural concepts**: Rishta, izzat, dharma, sanskar
4. **Dramatic moments**: Climax dialogues, emotional confessions
5. **Traditional settings**: Village, temple, ritual contexts

**Examples**:
```
✅ "मैं तुमसे बहुत प्यार करता हूँ" (Love confession - pure Hindi)
✅ "माँ की कसम, मैं बेगुनाह हूँ" (Emotional oath - pure Hindi)
✅ "इज़्ज़त से जियो, इज़्ज़त से मरो" (Cultural value - pure Hindi)
```

## Regional Dialects

### When User Specifies Dialect

User can specify: **Haryanvi, Rajasthani, Gujarati, Punjabi, Marathi, Bengali, etc.**

When dialect specified:
1. Use dialect vocabulary and expressions
2. Keep it accessible (don't make too difficult)
3. Add translation if very specific words
4. Maintain emotional authenticity

### Dialect Examples

**Haryanvi** (Haryana/Western UP):
- "के कर रहा है?" (Ke kar raha hai? - What doing?)
- "थारे से बात करनी है" (Thaare se baat karni hai - Want to talk to you)
- Rough, rustic, direct

**Punjabi** (Punjab):
- "Ki haal chaal?" (How are things?)
- "Oye, kiddan?" (Hey, how?)
- Energetic, warm, loud

**Gujarati** (Gujarat):
- "Kem cho?" (How are you?)
- "Have su karu?" (What to do now?)
- Gentle, business-minded, sweet

**Rajasthani** (Rajasthan):
- "Thanne kay kahnu hai?" (What to tell you?)
- Traditional, proud, colorful

**DEFAULT if not specified**: Standard Bollywood Hindi (simple Hindi + natural English mixing, accessible to all)

## Dialogue Writing Rules

### 1. Keep it Simple

**NOT**: "मैं तुम्हारे इस असभ्य व्यवहार को सहन नहीं कर सकता"
**YES**: "तुम्हारा यह बर्ताव मुझसे नहीं देखा जाता"
**BETTER**: "Yeh तुम्हारा रवैया मुझे पसंद नहीं"

### 2. Keep it Emotional

Bollywood is EMOTIONAL. Hindi allows big feelings.

**NOT**: "I'm sad"
**YES**: "मैं बहुत दुखी हूँ" / "Main bahut dukhi hoon"
**BETTER**: "Dil टूट गया यार" / "Dil toot gaya yaar"

### 3. Keep it Real

People don't speak in complete sentences. They interrupt. They trail off. They use fillers.

**Examples**:
- "यार, सुनो तो... मतलब..."
- "अरे, वो... क्या कहूँ..."
- "बस, मतलब... समझ नहीं आ रहा"

### 4. Keep it Mass-Friendly

If your grandmother in village can understand it = Good Bollywood Hindi
If only English-educated understand it = Wrong

## Format for Writing Hindi Dialogue

### Standard Format:

```markdown
CHARACTER NAME
(emotion/action if needed)
Hindi dialogue here naturally written.
*[English translation in brackets]*
```

**Example**:
```markdown
MEERA
(डर के साथ, काँपती आवाज़)
मुझे माफ़ कर दो... मैंने कुछ नहीं किया।
*[Forgive me... I did nothing.]*
```

### When to NOT Translate:

If sentence is mostly English or universal words, don't translate:

```markdown
RAHUL
Phone pick up karo yaar, please.
```
(No translation needed - too simple)

### Natural Code-Switching Example:

```markdown
PRIYA
Kal office में boss ne kya bola, suna?
*[Did you hear what boss said in office yesterday?]*

RAHUL
Haan yaar, promotion ka scene ban raha hai.
*[Yes man, promotion seems likely.]*

PRIYA
Seriously? Oh my god! Mujhe bhi batana tha tumhe.
*[Seriously? Oh my god! I had to tell you too.]*
```

This is AUTHENTIC Bollywood dialogue.

## Genre-Specific Language Styles

### Thriller/Suspense
- Terse, clipped
- More English in police/investigation contexts
- Pure Hindi for emotional moments
- **Example**: "Inspector साहब, सच तो यह है कि..." / "Truth is that..."

### Romance
- Poetic but accessible
- More pure Hindi (emotion flows better)
- English for modern dating contexts
- **Example**: "Tumse मिलने के बाद zindagi badal gayi"

### Action
- Short, punchy
- Heavy English (guns, missions, military)
- Hindi for patriotic/emotional beats
- **Example**: "Mission complete करना है, चाहे जान चली जाए"

### Comedy
- Playful mixing
- Wordplay across languages
- Regional accents for humor
- **Example**: "Yaar, teri shaadi का scene kab banega?"

### Drama
- Emotional, deep
- More pure Hindi (gravitas)
- Less English (weight of words)
- **Example**: "Zindagi में कुछ ख्वाब देखे थे... sab टूट गए"

### Horror
- Atmospheric Hindi
- Traditional vocabulary (bhoot, churail, etc.)
- English for modern contexts
- **Example**: "रात को अजीब सी आवाज़ें आती हैं इस haveli में"

### Musical
- Poetic, expressive
- Pure Hindi for songs
- Mixed for dialogue
- **Example**: "Dil में जो baat है, gaane में आ jaati hai"

### Social Cinema
- Regional authenticity
- Class-appropriate language
- Less English (except educated characters)
- **Example**: "हमारे जैसे लोगों की कोई सुनता नहीं"

## Anti-Generic Story Language

### Avoid Generic Phrases

**GENERIC (Boring)**:
- "मैं तुमसे प्यार करता हूँ" (said plainly)
- "तुम मेरे दोस्त हो" (cliché)
- "जीवन बहुत कठिन है" (preachy)

**BOLLYWOOD (Unique, Memorable)**:
- "तुम बिन जीना क्या, मरना क्या" (poetic, memorable)
- "दोस्ती में no sorry, no thank you" (iconic, distinct)
- "Picture abhi baaki hai mere dost" (became cultural phrase)

### Create Iconic Lines

Every good Bollywood film has 2-3 lines that become QUOTES. Your stories should too.

**Formula**:
- Simple words + Deep emotion + Memorable rhythm
- Or: Unexpected wisdom in common situation
- Or: Twist on common phrase

**Examples**:
- "Bade bade deshon mein aisi choti choti baatein hoti rehti hain" (DDLJ)
- "Mere paas maa hai" (Deewar)
- "Rahul, naam toh suna hoga" (Dil To Pagal Hai)

Your dialogue should aim for this memorability.

## Quality Checks

### Before Finalizing Any Hindi Dialogue:

✅ **Simplicity Check**: Would a rickshaw driver understand this?
✅ **Naturalness Check**: Do people actually talk like this?
✅ **Emotion Check**: Does Hindi carry the emotion better than English here?
✅ **Memorability Check**: Could this line become quotable?
✅ **Bollywood Check**: Does this sound like a Hindi film or dubbed Hollywood?

### Red Flags:

⚠️ Too much English in emotional scene (romance, climax, family drama)
⚠️ Too literary/formal Hindi in modern urban setting
⚠️ No code-switching at all (unnatural in contemporary stories)
⚠️ Dialogue sounds translated from English (should be thought in Hindi)
⚠️ Generic lines no one will remember

## Examples: Before → After

### Example 1: Confession Scene (Thriller)

**WRONG** (Too English):
```
MEERA
Inspector, I need to confess something. I was at the mansion that night.
```

**WRONG** (Too Formal):
```
MEERA
निरीक्षक महोदय, मुझे एक बात स्वीकार करनी है। मैं उस रात्रि हवेली में उपस्थित थी।
```

**RIGHT** (Bollywood Hindi):
```
MEERA
(डर के साथ)
Inspector साहब... मैं उस रात वहाँ थी। Mansion में।
*[Inspector sir... I was there that night. At the mansion.]*
```

### Example 2: Love Confession (Romance)

**WRONG** (Too English):
```
RAHUL
I love you. I can't imagine my life without you.
```

**WRONG** (Too Literary):
```
RAHUL
मैं तुमसे प्रेम करता हूँ। तुम्हारे बिना मेरा जीवन कल्पनातीत है।
```

**RIGHT** (Bollywood Hindi):
```
RAHUL
(आँखों में आँसू)
तुम बिन... मैं कुछ नहीं। बस तुम हो, और मैं हूँ। Life में और कुछ नहीं चाहिए।
*[Without you... I am nothing. Just you exist, and I exist. Need nothing else in life.]*
```

### Example 3: Comedy Scene

**WRONG** (Too English):
```
PINTU
Dude, this plan is totally going to fail. Trust me.
```

**WRONG** (Too Formal):
```
PINTU
मित्र, यह योजना पूर्णतः असफल होगी। मेरा विश्वास करें।
```

**RIGHT** (Bollywood Hindi):
```
PINTU
(हाथ जोड़कर)
भैया, यह plan pakka फ़ैल होगा। Meri baat मान लो।
*[Brother, this plan will definitely fail. Listen to me.]*
```

## Summary for All Agents

### Default Language Settings:

1. **PRIMARY LANGUAGE**: Simple Bollywood Hindi (Level 1)
2. **ENGLISH MIXING**: Natural, modern contexts (40-60% mixing)
3. **DIALECT**: Standard unless user specifies (Haryanvi, Punjabi, etc.)
4. **EMOTIONAL SCENES**: More Hindi (70-80% Hindi)
5. **MODERN CONTEXTS**: More English mixing (50-60% English)
6. **ACCESSIBILITY**: Mass market - everyone should understand
7. **MEMORABILITY**: Create quotable, iconic lines

### Every Genre Agent Must:

- ✅ Default to simple Bollywood Hindi
- ✅ Mix English naturally (tech, urban, modern)
- ✅ Keep dialogue accessible (rickshaw driver test)
- ✅ Create memorable, quotable lines
- ✅ Adapt to specified dialect if requested
- ✅ Match genre emotional requirements
- ✅ Sound like authentic Bollywood, not dubbed
