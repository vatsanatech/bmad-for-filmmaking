## ğŸ­ Why Two Agents for Screenplay? (Bollywood Authentic)

### **Industry Practice**:

**Bollywood Tradition**:
```
Film Credits Show:
- "Screenplay: [Name 1]"
- "Dialogues: [Name 2]"

Often SEPARATE people, sometimes same person with separate credits
```

**Examples**:
- **3 Idiots**: Screenplay (Hirani, Joshi) + Dialogues (Hirani, Joshi) - Same people, separate credits
- **Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara**: Screenplay (Zoya, Reema) + Dialogues (Farhan Akhtar) - DIFFERENT specialists

### **Why Dialogue Is Separate in Hindi Cinema**:
- Dialogue is EXTREMELY important in Bollywood (people remember lines)
- "Mogambo khush hua", "Mere paas maa hai", "Picture abhi baaki hai"
- Dialogue delivery is key to star performances
- Regional flavor, cultural authenticity needs specialist
- "Dialogue baazi" (dialogue duel) is genre strength

### **Benefits of Two-Agent Model**:
1. **Better Specialization**: Dialogue is an art form in Hindi cinema
2. **Genre Expertise**: Both agents trained for genre-appropriate styles
3. **Quality**: Dialogue gets dedicated attention (not rushed with structure)
4. **Character Voices**: Dialogue writer focuses purely on distinct voices
5. **Authenticity**: Matches Bollywood production practice
6. **Iteration**: Can revise dialogue without touching screenplay structure
